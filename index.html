05 — Financial Assistance

Education Loans & Scholarships

State-wise student credit card schemes and university scholarships.

Bihar Student Credit Card Scheme (DRCC)

The Bihar government provides an education loan of up to ₹4 Lakhs at a nominal interest rate (4% general, 1% for girls/disabled) to help students pursue higher education.

Eligibility

  • Permanent resident of Bihar.
  • Passed 12th standard (Intermediate).
  • Age 25 years or younger.

Key Features

  • Up to ₹4 Lakhs loan amount.
  • Used for tuition, living, books, and laptop.
  • Simple application via MNSSBY portal and DRCC visit.

Jharkhand Guruji Student Credit Card Scheme

A visionary scheme by the Jharkhand Government offering collateral-free education loans up to ₹15 Lakhs at just 4% simple interest.

Eligibility

  • Permanent resident of Jharkhand.
  • Passed Class 10/12 from a recognized school in Jharkhand.
  • Maximum age of 40 years.

Key Features

  • Up to ₹15 Lakhs (70% fees, 30% living).
  • Collateral-free (Govt acts as guarantor).
  • 15 years repayment period with 1-year moratorium.

Madhya Pradesh Education Loan Schemes

Schemes like the Mukhyamantri Medhavi Vidyarthi Yojana (MMVY) and other state education loans support meritorious students from MP.

Eligibility (General)

  • Resident of Madhya Pradesh.
  • Good academic record (e.g., 70%+ in MP Board or 85%+ in CBSE/ICSE for MMVY).
  • Family income limits may apply depending on the specific scheme.

Key Features

  • Financial assistance for technical and professional courses.
  • Subsidized interest rates or full fee waiver under MMVY.

Standard Bank Education Loan (SBI, HDFC, etc.)

Comprehensive guidance for education loans from any nationalized or private bank. Below is the complete checklist of required documents.

1. Education Documents

  • Admission letter & ID Card
  • 10th & 12th Certificate
  • Result - Semester wise
  • Entrance Exam Result
  • Gap Certificate (if applicable)
  • Copy of Confirming scholarship & Fee slip
  • Schedule of expenses
  • Collateral Documents

2. KYC, Income & Additional Docs

  • KYC: PAN + Aadhar
  • Income (Co-applicant): Income Certificate, Salary slip (Last 3 Month), Form 16 / IT Return, Bank Statements
  • Additional: Application Form, Admission & Completion Date, Business Activity proof.
  • Expenses Covered: Tuition fee, Exam fee, Stationery, Computer, Hostel, Tour/Travels, 2 Wheeler.
